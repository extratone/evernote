Actual summarized transcript:


1.  Where are you from?

-Auxvasse, MO


  1. What’s your technical position here?

-”General Sales Manger.”


  1. How long have you been working for University Subaru?  How long in this particular position?

-6 years for University Subaru 

-4 years as General Sales Manager


  1. Was this your first job in the automotive industry?

-Retired from previous job in insurance financial planning.

-Then hired by University Subaru as a consultant.

-Insurance agent for 22 years

-”Owned my own business”

-Participated in an early retirement program 

-Acquaintances with owners, led to job as a consultant, which eventually led to job as a full-time manager


5.  Who owns University Subaru?

-Dave Drane and Dan Burks


  1. What’s it like selling cars in Columbia, given the continuing expansion of the Joe Machens Group?

-”They have a monopoly, there’s no doubt.”

-”It depends on what your selling.”

-”It doesn’t effect us because we have a niche buy.”

-”Subaru buyers are extremely loyal.”


-Symmetrical all-wheel drive

-Loyal client base

-Makes a difference in the pre-owned cars given that there are varying makes in the used car market

-”What brings people back here is a niche buy.”

-Different angle than other manufacturers


  1. New customers must make up some of your market.

-”You have to go out and conquer that.”

-”Our market is consistent.”

-”Our direct competition is Honda and Toyota.”


-The location of University Subaru is especially integral to their market.  They are the only Subaru dealer within 125 miles, whereas almost ever other manufacturer is represented in several nearby cities (Jefferson City, Mexico, Fulton, etc.)

-Though University Subaru is very far from any other Subaru dealer, they do attempt to “conquer” the market of the other Missouri dealers.


  1. Is it the all-wheel drive, then, that yields the Subaru niche?

“Three things”:

-Boxer engine.  Subaru and Porsche are the only manufactures with passenger vehicles powered by Boxer engines currently on the market.  Yields a low center of gravity.  Increases safety, as the engine is designed to travel under the firewall in a collision.

-Subaru is the only manufacturer that uses “symmetrical all-wheel drive.”

“Four suction cups on the road.”

-Safety.  “One of, if not the safest cars on the road, and they have been for 10 years in a row.”  

“So you’re talking safety, performance, and it’s not unusual to see Subarus running around with 300,000-400,000 miles on them.”


“They’re long lasting, dependable, reliable, and they are very safe.”


-”Since ‘they’ came out with the CVT transmission, customers want to compare them to the Civic and Corolla.”

-Comparing with Civic and Corolla, price and mileage are the same, but the symmetrical all-wheel drive is an extremely important difference (and selling point.)


  1. Given that we were talking about Subaru’s niche in the market due in part to all-wheel drive, how has the release of the BRZ come in to play, specifically for this dealership?

-The BRZ does not fit with the traditional Subaru buyer because it is rear-wheel drive.

-Discussing competition between the FR-S and BRZ, Nixon notes the Scion badge as evidence enough.

-In comparison, the suspension of the BRZ is “more Subaru....tighter” than the FR-S.

-People ask why they should choose the BRZ over the FR-S, Nixon notes that Subaru has led the industry in residual value, with a figure of 61%.  (Does this apply, given what was said.  The BRZ does not possess Subaru’s traditional values, so why would the increase in “residual value” apply?)

-”If you buy the Scion [FR-S], which is essentially the same car, and then you wreck it, your insurance company is not going to give you as much than if you had the Subaru [BRZ] because it holds its value better.”


-”The badge means a lot.”


-”It’s just statistics.”


  1. Since the release of the Toyota GT86/Scion FR-S/Subaru BRZ, I’ve seen many more Scions in this area than BRZs?

-Essentially, it’s a volume issue.  Scion dealerships in this area have a much larger presence.

-”We’ve had a total of 5 here in the store, since it came out.  All 5 were pre-sold.” 

“Subaru, that’s the way they do their vehicles” (referring to pre-selling vs. Toyota’s “mass production.”) 


“If you don’t have it, it’s hard to describe to people why they should buy it, and that’s a challenge that we fight.”


  1. What’s your daily driver?

-Richard drives a Legacy, also owns a Tribeca.

-Son drives an Impreza.
