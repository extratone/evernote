A note on Secret Screenings:

This year, True/False will show three Secret Screenings. While we encourage everyone to immerse themselves in the mystique of the Secret Screenings, it’s crucial to note that those films are permanently embargoed.

This means that these films cannot be written about in any way in the context of True/False.

The media and audience members are accountable to not mention these films in any form, including on social media. True/False staff will be screening social media, blogs, and other outlets throughout the fest for any illicit mention of a secret screening. Should someone break this rule, he or she will not be allowed to return to True/False (and will be banished to our gulag in the deepest Ozarks). It is okay to publish reviews of Secret Screening films AFTER they have officially premiered elsewhere. However, True/False still cannot be mentioned or even alluded to in relation to the film or the location in which it was seen.

True/False’s ability to play Secret Screenings in the future depends on your discretion, as we maintain close — but quite vulnerable — relationships with other festivals. We believe that this unique component of our fest not only helps our audience but also gives writers like you a leg up on writing pieces later this spring after these films officially premiere at other festivals. We appreciate your cooperation.

We also ask that you refrain from writing full reviews of Manifesto (capsule reviews are OK) until its theatrical release.

How does your pass work?

The Media pass allows you to “Q” for any film for free. Simply line up an hour to an hour and a half before the screening you want to see. One hour before the start of the show, you will receive a number. You are then welcome to go off and explore Columbia, grab a coffee, etc. —  just make sure you are back to the venue 15 minutes before showtime. At that point, resume your place in the queue, and the venue captain will begin to release available seats. If there are 15 seats left, 1 through 15 in the "Q" will get in, etc. BE SURE TO RETURN ON TIME. If you are late and miss your Q number being called, you will likely have to go to the back of the line.

The Media pass will also allow you to attend Gimme Truth! and Campfire Stories.

Among other events and fest features you are encouraged to highlight and experience:

·  FIELD SESSIONS (Little Chapel at The Picturehouse): The sessions jettison the traditional festival panel format for intimate one-on-one dialogues between filmmakers.

·  CHAUTAUQUA (Rhynsburger Theatre, Sunday, 9:30am): A variety show bringing together all of the elements of the weekend through provocations, music, and storytelling

·  VR ARCADE (Columbia Art League, next door to the Missouri Theatre): Carefully curated selection of the very best new interactive and virtual reality projects. Sponsored by Good Wizard.

Be sure to keep your Media pass on you at ALL TIMES to ensure your entrance into films and events.
