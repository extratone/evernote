101 mph

start at 0 miles per hour.

another face at the bar,

another coffee cup to fill,

another body to banter with.

someone else unreachable.

someone else unattainable.

suddenly i’m going 10 in a school zone,

hands clutched on the steering wheel

i’ve got to stop myself

lest i spin out of control

driving down the line between

        love and infatuation

trying to stay in my lane

begging my heart to keep itself in check

25 miles per hour and i’m wearing your jacket

as you blow nicotine into my lungs,

laughing at nothing,

all i can think about is holding your hand

climbing into your arms,

stealing the heat of your body,

trying to steal your heart.

i look at the speedometer,

and then at the speed limit sign.

i’m only going 5 miles over

but i’ll need to watch myself

suddenly i’m flirting on purpose

and i think you’re flirting back

and there’s something in your eyes

        each time you look at me

a fondness i cannot name,

a wryness i cannot touch,

because at 40 miles per hour i cannot reach you.

you’re speeding too far ahead

i am not brave enough to drive faster.

i am not brave enough to reach my limit

but i am beginning to imagine a world where

        the limit does not exist.

so i ask you to get drunk with me

        (ever the romantic,)

and hope you’re reading between the same lines

suddenly i am hurtling down the freeway

gripping tightly at the wheel

70 miles per hour on the speedometer,

you are saying all the right things

and i can only hope i’m replying in turn

as we leave the bar,

and i reach for your hand,

a piece of me settles,

as another piece of me starts to open.

i have not given my heart in so long

that a layer of dust is flying

        as i pull it off the shelf

hurriedly making sure it’s all right

        that the tape is holding,

and i can hand it to you.

you’re still ahead of me on the road

yet for once i am gaining,

if not on you than on myself

if not on you than on the horizon

suddenly i am following you around backroad curves

        90 miles an hour and gaining

while at the same time you’re hovering over me

mouth on my stomach and heading down

i grasp at your hands as you map the uncharted

and we are no longer driving,

we are flying

i have finally reached you

and you are reaching back

there’s religion in this,        

even if we have no god

there’s holiness in this moment,

and i have never felt more worshipped

my ankles clasped behind your head

more tightly than my hands

if there is still a limit,

i have elected to ignore it

trading common sense for intuition

and as my engine overheats,

still flying over the pavement,

while my head rests on your chest

        a contradiction of speed and stillness,

my heart is still racing

as your breath sets a slow rhythm.

laughter trickles out of me from deep inside

a levy breaking that has not ever faltered

a freedom that has never been felt.

even if i spin out of control not

careen off the freeway and into the embankment,

i don’t want to stop our forward trajectory

i don’t know which side of the road i’m on

or if i’m still the one driving, even,

but i don’t want to slow down

the stars are clear in the night sky

orion stark above us

and the wind roars around us like a cocoon

everything is perfect

        everything is perfect

101 miles per hour
