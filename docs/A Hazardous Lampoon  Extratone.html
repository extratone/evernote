            A Hazardous Lampoon

            •February 13, 2017
            Written by K. I. Eleutherios XVII

            The Tump Abnormistration is a perfect example of something akin to satire being thrown into high power.

            Remember that movie a while back called Man of the Year with Robin Williams playing a Jon Stewart-kind of satirist who ends up winning the Presidency?
            (There's also a bit of an overblown subplot concerning absurd algorithms based on double consonants.)

            Tump is like the dark, biting humor version instead of the cheery fun humor version. But satire needs a veil of safety, which is usually afforded through its fictional nature.

            Now, the pyrotechnic jokes are real, and the rifles being shot into the audience are loaded not with blanks but with hollowpoints. (Vacant in the head.)

            Maybe it's what the country needs - to snap back in the direction of progress.

            A rude awakening.

            But at the same time, it's quite a strain on the rubber band of functional and civil civilization. If it snaps, it's all over. And maybe we do need another rubber band, but rubber bands this big come at quite an expense.

            Rights, time, safety, lives.

            This is the darkest satire, and Poe's not far behind to cover its tracks.

            There is a concept that has been around since the 70s called Operation Mindfuck among the Discordians and Erisians in the world and perhaps just "culturejamming" by everyone else.

            It's defined by satire and parody of fact to wake the general population up, and to get people to start questioning everything. It was for a time when people took fact for granted.

            Currently, there are some Discordians who are calling for "Operation Mindfix" - a reversal of OM - because the climate of truth has been so muddled that the change - the awakening - is actually approaching objective fact.

            On a classic approach to the seasons of chaos, this could very well be a sign of waning Bureaucracy leading to the Aftermath, which won't be pretty.

             

            Though it also may very well play out like an episode of Saturday Night Live that makes the population of the United States vomit synchronously into their late night snacks.
