iOS 10 Review

David Blue on September 10, 2016

Thanks to this week’s Sep­tem­ber 2016 Keynote, we now know that iOS 10 will be offi­cial­ly released in just three days. I sup­pose it was a bit sil­ly of me to down­load the pub­lic beta so late in the game, but I did, and regret it much less than I could’ve imag­ined. I’ve used my rose gold iPhone 6S Plus more than any per­son­al device I’ve ever owned. For the first time in my func­tion­ing life, I’ve had no per­sis­tent need for a lap­top, which is a pret­ty big deal for rea­sons you couldn’t care less about.

Own­er­ship of my first gen­er­a­tion iPhone trained me to dread new releas­es. Expect­ed­ly, it got less and less usable every time Ste­vie took the stage. For relevance’s sake, I decid­ed to opt in a bit ear­ly, despite my assump­tion that my 6S Plus would show its first signs of strug­gle as a result.

Imme­di­ate­ly, though, the oppo­site effect was notice­able. Every­thing is faster: noti­fi­ca­tions, app opening/​closing, intra-app func­tion, and text entry, even. Smoother, too — so much so, it’s almost a shame we’ve had to use iOS 9 on these devices for a whole year. The effect of such smooth­ing on my per­cep­tion should be kept in con­sid­er­a­tion while read­ing the rest of my opin­ing.

Raise to wake is less tedious/​more use­ful than I antic­i­pat­ed, though it’s been briefly mis­trig­gered a few times in tran­sit from inside my Moshi Over­ture Wal­let Case (an extra­or­di­nary prod­uct, by the way,) but it should be not­ed that I walk rather strange­ly. With near-default sleep/​wake set­tings, I did not notice enough instances to have quan­tifi­able pow­er con­se­quences.

Mes­sages has been made appro­pri­ate­ly com­plex for the times. The pres­sure to “com­pete” with Snapchat, Face­book Mes­sen­ger, What’sApp, etc. is under­stand­able, but the exe­cu­tion of their response is not. I can­not per­son­al­ly imag­ine a rea­son­able addi­tion to SMS. Its role is pure­ly func­tion­al. I wasn’t able to try out every new fea­ture. Not just because I don’t know any­one with iOS 10, but because — crit­i­cal­ly — I could not think of some­one I’d be will­ing to both­er with use­less garbage like doo­dles. And even if I could’ve, it’d have been less trou­ble to make use of one of those Third-Par­ty Appli­ca­tions from the Soft­ware Shop that Apple was so proud of on Wednes­day. For the elder­ly like myself, even clev­er­ly-stowed but­tons for unused fea­tures are still unus­able but­tons. The alter­ations feel a bit more oblig­a­tory; less vision­ary. It’s a shame that Apple is los­ing its aware­ness of the appro­pri­ate time for one, the oth­er, or nei­ther.

Com­pe­tence in Apple cov­er­age is heav­i­ly depen­dent upon the abil­i­ty to dif­fer­en­ti­ate between the allure of an intel­li­gent­ly blingy addi­tion and the allure of an intel­li­gent­ly func­tion­al one. (For­give the crude lan­guage.)

Con­tex­tu­al pre­dic­tions are impres­sive and sur­pris­ing­ly clever. It’s encour­ag­ing to see progress in such sim­ple inter­ac­tions, but — be hon­est with your­self — mak­ing use of it is going to cost you time in most sit­u­a­tions. Hate to be exces­sive­ly meta, but keep in mind — again — that I am not the major­i­ty. I tend to enjoy typ­ing things out, ya know.

Please leave a com­ment if you think my vision’s a bit obscured because of things like this. Dis­course is the hand­i­est secu­ri­ty.

To be hon­est, I’ve loved 3D Touch all along, even though some­thing like 75% of my trig­gers were unin­ten­tion­al. It hap­pens to be very use­ful for grab­bing images from the web, if you’re into that sort of thing. Ulti­mate­ly, I just enjoy the sen­sa­tion. The curt vibra­tion and brit­tle illu­so­ry third inter­ac­tive dimen­sion have pro­vid­ed more val­ue for me than actu­al func­tion­al­i­ty.

With touch and go, though, I can instant­ly con­jure a con­cise sum­ma­ry of cur­rent local weath­er con­di­tions, or a sin­gu­lar sto­ry from Apple News. It’s going to take con­scious effort to remem­ber to use them, yeah, but anything’s a plus when your love for some­thing is already lack­ing ratio­nal­i­ty.

As to Apple News itself… It’s not the same appli­ca­tion. The “For You” roll was amus­ing­ly messy before the update, but actu­al struc­ture (read: func­tion­al aggre­ga­tion) has done away with its beau­ty, I’m afraid. It is somuch faster, though. I guess we’ll have to take it seri­ous­ly, now.

I haven’t got­ten the oppor­tu­ni­ty to real­ly uti­lize rich noti­fi­ca­tions, but I can’t imag­ine any tan­gi­ble increase in your func­tion. Per­haps sin­gu­lar­ly, I’ve always found my habit­u­al nav­i­ga­tion to the appli­ca­tions them­selves to be con­sis­tent­ly quick enough to debunk “no, you don’t have to leave the app” fea­tures.

My favorite addi­tion is the abil­i­ty to search pho­tos by con­tent. A few tri­als demon­strate that it can differ­en­ti­ate between night­fall and night; clothed and nude. (I have nev­er been naked.) It’s the most intrigu­ing, too, because it means more nuanced & contro­ver­sial image-recog­nized search vari­ables are getting closer and closer to mar­ket.

Search by expres­sion? How about emo­tion, even?

I can’t wait until the whole Apple orga­ni­za­tion must delib­er­ate the per­ceived dif­fer­ences between peace and apa­thy with­in an algo­rith­mic con­text.

“Please define ‘joy,’ Mr. Ive.”
